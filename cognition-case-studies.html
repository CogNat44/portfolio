The Challenge

With no HVAC visibility or control across hundreds of Canadian locations, this national auto parts distributor had no way to identify, act on, or measure energy savings. Maintenance was entirely reactive — wait for something to break, then fix it.

Simple Rollout at Scale

Compared to competing solutions, Cognition was significantly easier to install. The Cognition team provided rollout project management support, and built-in cellular connectivity worked out of the box — no Wi-Fi configuration, no IT tickets, no network security negotiations.

A single HVAC technician could complete multiple location installs per day, with hardware for both HVAC and refrigeration paired and configured from a smartphone. The result: installation savings of approximately $75 per unit across the portfolio.

Visibility That Changed Behavior

Before Cognition, thermostats across all locations were set to a permanent hold — meaning HVAC equipment ran continuously, regardless of occupancy or time of day. The Cognition Dashboard gave users the tools to create flexible schedules, see when those schedules were overridden, and hold locations accountable for unnecessary usage.

Where problematic patterns persisted, the distributor's head office used Cognition's remote lockout feature to enforce appropriate settings centrally.

Since deploying Cognition, average HVAC runtime is down 20% across locations — translating to 8–11% savings on utility costs.

Centralized Information Management

Cognition also addressed a persistent operational gap: fragmented, inconsistent records about equipment across hundreds of locations. Equipment notes make it easy to log service calls, configuration changes, and maintenance history — accessible to both store-level staff and head office.

Utility bills are uploaded directly to the Dashboard for quick access and ongoing validation of energy savings, giving the distributor a single source of truth across the portfolio.

The Challenge

The Municipality manages a portfolio of small public buildings spread across a largely rural landscape of villages and hamlets. Without data, every HVAC issue required a physical site visit — even for simple tasks like seasonal temperature adjustments. The Municipality needed remote visibility and control, but full Building Automation Systems weren't a viable option given the cost and complexity involved.

Straightforward Installation — Even in Remote Locations

Cognition was installed by the Municipality's local HVAC partner. Cellular connectivity worked out of the box across all sites, including more remote locations — providing reliable data transmission without any networking infrastructure investment.

From Reactive to Proactive

Before Cognition, issues in public buildings only came to light when they were already emergencies — when spaces were too cold to use, or equipment had failed entirely. Cognition alerts gave the Municipality early warning, enabling action before residents were affected.

Cognition Dashboard access is now shared with the Municipality's local HVAC partners, who can remotely review data, triage issues, and advise on whether a site visit is warranted — reducing unnecessary truck rolls and improving response times.

In Action: Community Hall Heating Failure

On a Saturday morning in October, Cognition detected a heating malfunction in the Community Hall auditorium, alerting the Facility Director and HVAC technician simultaneously.

The team reviewed dashboard data remotely, confirmed a site visit was needed, and dispatched a technician who found and fixed a faulty gas line connection. Data confirmed the issue was resolved — and the building was cleared for its scheduled reservations without disruption.

One Platform for the Whole Portfolio

Cognition's built-in information management tools — equipment notes, configuration history, photos, and utility bill uploads — ensure that every decision-maker, from facilities staff to HVAC partners, has the context they need to act quickly and correctly.

For a small municipality managing a large geographic spread with limited staff, that shared visibility makes the difference between a resolved issue and a failed public event.

The Challenge

Dealership staff had almost no tools to manage aging HVAC equipment across a complex, mixed-use building — mechanic bays with large garage doors, private offices, and customer-facing areas all in one. With limited technical knowledge onsite and no equipment data available, frustration with comfort and reliability was high.

Flexible Hardware for Complex Spaces

The dealership's building presented specific challenges. In the mechanic bays, large garage doors opened and closed frequently, constantly importing outside air. In staff offices, thermostats were poorly positioned — so the temperatures driving HVAC decisions didn't reflect what staff actually experienced.

Cognition Smart Commercial Controls were compatible with the dealership's varied HVAC equipment, and wireless sensors were installed in problem areas to improve temperature accuracy — improving comfort while reducing unnecessary equipment runtime.

Control That's Actually Usable

Using Cognition's web-based Dashboard and flexible permissions model, staff can now manage comfort and equipment settings from their desktop — without needing to be in front of a thermostat. All override activity is logged automatically, making it easy to identify problem patterns.

Cognition Intelligence also auto-limits override durations to prevent runaway comfort adjustments from undermining energy savings. The result: improved comfort alongside a ~20% reduction in HVAC runtime and lower utility bills.

Equipment Intelligence That Pays for Itself

Within weeks of installation, Cognition's runtime and temperature analytics began surfacing issues that had previously gone undetected:

  • Capacity limits on aging equipment — older units struggling to reach setpoints during warm weather, helping the dealership prioritize where to invest in upgrades
  • Equipment degradation signals — early indicators of units requiring service, before failures occurred
  • Isolated power surges — an ongoing pattern that helped the dealership's electrician diagnose and fix an underlying electrical issue
  • Preventive maintenance reminders — automated air filter change notifications to stay ahead of performance degradation

All alerts and reminders are auto-documented in the Dashboard's Equipment Notes, giving the dealership a running maintenance log shared with their HVAC service provider.

Smart Freezer Monitoring Catches What Manual Checks Miss

QSR Restaurant Walk-In Freezer Predictive Alerting
Weeks
Earlier detection compared to manual temperature checks
Avoided
Catastrophic equipment failure and food spoilage
Zero
False alarm fatigue — signal-based alerts only
The Challenge

Freezer equipment failures tend to develop gradually — but manual temperature checks and on-floor staff rarely catch the early signs before things become emergencies. Worse, many monitoring solutions generate so many false alarms that alerts get ignored entirely. The result: equipment issues escalate into disruptions, food spoilage, and expensive emergency repair calls.

Monitoring That Knows the Difference

Cognition's alerting logic is built to understand normal freezer behavior — including defrost cycles, door opens during restocking, and routine temperature variation — before flagging a problem. Alerts fire when patterns deviate from the norm in ways that actually matter, not as a reflex to every temperature blip.

The Walk-In Freezer: A Step-by-Step Story

A

Cognition data showed the walk-in freezer consistently operating at the upper end of its normal range. Defrost cycles briefly pushed temperatures out of range, but the overall picture was within acceptable bounds — no alerts, no action needed.

B

Machine learning algorithms continued to factor in defrost cycles and door open events when evaluating alert thresholds — notifying staff only when onsite practices drove extended out-of-range periods, without unnecessary noise.

C

Smart trending alerts identified a sustained upward shift in the freezer's operating temperature — the kind of gradual change that's invisible to manual checks, but unmistakable in continuous data. Onsite staff and HQ were both notified.

D

Onsite staff pushed back, insisting temperatures were fine. Cognition data gave HQ the confidence to dispatch a service call anyway. The technician found a nearly empty sight glass, a clogged condenser coil, and low refrigerant — equipment issues that would have continued degrading silently until a full failure.

Service Report — From the Technician

"The freezer was running but the temperature was high. Found the sight glass almost empty. Checked the condenser coil — it was completely clogged. Cleaned it and tested operation. Still running, but refrigerant level was low. Recommended leak search and repair."

Without Cognition's trending intelligence, this freezer would have continued operating in a degraded state until a failure — with no warning, and no time to plan.
